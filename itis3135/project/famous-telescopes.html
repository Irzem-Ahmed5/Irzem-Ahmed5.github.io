Famous Large-scale Telescopes

Hubble Space Telescope

The Hubble Space Telescope is a Cassegrain reflector telescope satellite designed to view the universe without the interference of earth's atomosphere. It was launced in 1990 and received repairs from Dec 1993 to May 2009. It was discovered to have a tiny flaw, which caused its images to be blurred. However, in 1993 astronauts repaired it by essentially adding a material that acted as eyeglasses for the telescope. (Source)

James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope, launched on December 25, 2021, is the largest telescope in space. It is meant to help view those sky objects that are too distant or faint for the Hubble Space telescope to view. It aims to enable us to see things previously opaque to the Hubble Space telescope. It may also give more detail of potentially inhabitable planets.
