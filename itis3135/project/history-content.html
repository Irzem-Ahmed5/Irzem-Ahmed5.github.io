Astronomy in the Ancient times

A long long time ago, Astronomy entailed the observation of sky objects only visible to the naked eye. This information was used for religious, ceremonial, or even agricultural purposes. Babylonian contributions came earlier and built the base of the subject. After them Greek astronomers spearheaded the most progess. (Source)

Astronomy through the middle ages

In the middles ages, astronomers in Islamic lands had mastered the Greek accomplishments and progressed it further. Arabic had become the language of learning astronomy, just as Greek was before it. After the revival of education in Europe, the leading language then became latin, which was translated first from Arabic translations of Greek works, until the European astronomers got direct access to those Greek works. Astronomy was also studied extensively in India and China. Thus, astronomy became a subject studied across different languages and cultures across the world. (Source)
